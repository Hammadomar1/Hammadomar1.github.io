About Me

I am a PhD Student at UT Dallas in Computer Engineering working on neuromorphic, embodied AI for real-time robotic control, Real-Time Autonomous Systems, and Safe Reactive Robotics. I build neuro-symbolic systems that achieve fast, robust, and interpretable sensorimotor behavior on edge hardware. Interested in research collaborations and industry internships spanning robotics, edge AI, and hardware-software co-design.

My research centers on embodied AI, robust perception-to-control, and simulation-driven learning for autonomous systems. I design, implement, and evaluate Python/C++ algorithms for multimodal sensing, control, and robustness under clutter, occlusion, latency, and distribution shift.

Research Contributions

ENACT: Ensemble Neural Fields for Reactive Robot Control

Accepted at DAC 2026

Designed a neuromorphic reactive-control approach based on ensemble neural fields for fast and robust robot behavior. This work focuses on real-time perception-to-control, stable attractor dynamics, and interpretable sensorimotor decisions for embodied autonomy.

Two-Time-Scale Reactive Autonomy for Embodied Control

Developed a real-time reflex-control architecture with asynchronous advisory adaptation, integrating simulation-based evaluation, safety-aware control, and robustness analysis under delay and dropout.

Shift-Aware Transfer Learning for Robust Robot Policies from Simulation

Adapted simulation-trained policies to real-world settings and evaluated robustness to clutter, occlusion, latency, and dynamics shifts.
